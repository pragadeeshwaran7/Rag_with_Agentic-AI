# CBSE Class 10 Science - Previous Year Questions (PYQ) Reference

This document contains actual patterns and question examples from recent CBSE Class 10 Science Board Examinations. It is used by the AI to ensure the generated questions match the exact cognitive difficulty and phrasing of the real exam.

## Section A: Objective Type (MCQs)
*Focus: Direct recall, simple application, and observation.*
- **PYQ Example 1:** In an experiment to trace the path of a ray of light through a glass prism, the angle of incidence (i) and angle of emergence (e) are related as: (a) i > e (b) i < e (c) i = e (d) unpredictable.
- **PYQ Example 2:** Which of the following is a balanced chemical equation for the action of steam on iron? (a) 3Fe + 4H2O → Fe3O4 + 4H2 (b) Fe + H2O → FeO + H2.

## Section B: Very Short Answer (2 Marks)
*Focus: Definitions, brief explanations, and simple numericals.*
- **PYQ Example 1:** State the two laws of reflection of light. 
- **PYQ Example 2:** Why are decomposition reactions called the opposite of combination reactions? Write equations for these reactions.
- **PYQ Example 3:** Trace the path of air in the human respiratory system.

## Section C: Short Answer (3 Marks)
*Focus: Multi-step explanations, balancing equations with conditions, and comparative analysis.*
- **PYQ Example 1:** (a) What is a homologous series of carbon compounds? (b) Write the name and formula of the 2nd member of the homologous series having general formula CnH2n.
- **PYQ Example 2:** Draw a schematic diagram of a circuit consisting of a battery of three cells of 2V each, a 5 ohm resistor, an 8 ohm resistor, and a 12 ohm resistor, and a plug key, all connected in series. Calculate the current through the circuit.

## Section D: Long Answer (5 Marks)
*Focus: Complex phenomena, detailed biological processes, and multi-part physics problems.*
- **PYQ Example 1:** (a) Explain the process of double circulation in the human heart. (b) Why is it necessary to separate oxygenated and deoxygenated blood in mammals and birds?
- **PYQ Example 2:** (a) State Ohm's Law. (b) How does the resistance of a wire depend on its length and cross-sectional area? (c) Two wires of the same material have lengths in the ratio 1:2 and radii in the ratio 2:1. Compare their resistances.

## Section E: Case-Based / Competency-Based (4 Marks)
*Focus: Analytical thinking applied to real-world scenarios or experiments.*
- **PYQ Case:** A student takes 2g of silver chloride in a china dish and places it in sunlight for some time. He observes that the white silver chloride turns grey. 
  - (i) Identify the type of chemical reaction taking place. (1 Mark)
  - (ii) Write a balanced chemical equation for the reaction. (1 Mark)
  - (iii) State one commercial application of this type of reaction. (2 Marks)
